# CAVE information hierarchy audit

## Decision rule

An item stays permanently visible only when it changes the next experimental decision. Method traceability remains available, but repeated source labels, solver notes and status narration must not compete with evidence.

| Screen | Must remain visible | Demote or reveal on demand | Remove from the permanent surface |
|---|---|---|---|
| Global | Current case, active lifecycle stage | Gap/interpolation warning only when present | Repeated 24 h window and modelled ACH from the global status strip; both already appear in their working screens |
| Challenge | Two event choices, measured profile, selected 24 h window, peak and timestamp, primary handoff | Instrument and source in one compact provenance line | Repeated selected-window card chart and the explanatory sentence that restates the measured boundary |
| Design | Building, envelope, ventilation, filter, temperature, spatial model, three response outcomes | Full equation and parameter table behind a compact model-details disclosure; protocol steps behind Plan sheet | Repeated model provenance chips and always-visible four-step experiment trace |
| Run | Playback control, current time, compact spatial context, selected/key sensors, outdoor/indoor response, peak and lag | Full sensor register through the spatial model/Plan sheet; solver and interpolation details outside the primary surface | Cross-unit “severity ranking”, all-systems-nominal log, repeated solver prose, oversized 3D reconstruction chrome |
| Analyse | Three headline results and the recommended protection strategy | Rule trace and method statement in a disclosure/export pack | Repeated provenance badges and low-level fitting metadata from the permanent canvas |

## Run composition decision

The Run screen is an evidence replay, not a 3D showcase. The timeline becomes a compact utility row. The spatial reconstruction receives a fixed, shallow band sufficient to orient the building and sensor positions. Four representative sensors are shown without ranking incompatible units. The concentration-response panel receives the largest share of the screen because it explains the experiment, and a compact run summary replaces the protocol-status card.

At 1440×810 the target allocation is approximately 12% timeline, 31% spatial context, and 57% response evidence. At 1920×1080 the spatial region remains capped rather than expanding with viewport height.
