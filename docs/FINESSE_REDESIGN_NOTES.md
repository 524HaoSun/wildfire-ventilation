## Applicable guidance

The CAVE workbench needs two registers. The entering page is a restrained **brand surface** whose job is first impression and orientation. The five workbench stages are **product/workflow UI** whose job is clarity, density and task completion. The brand grammar must not leak into the operational screens: no giant hero type, grain-heavy decoration or spectacle motion inside the workbench.

For the product layer, the most important substrate decisions are tinted neutrals, a locked accent, hairline or absent borders, hue-tinted whisper shadows, a fixed type scale and feedback-only motion. The workbench should use one strong floating shell rather than dozens of hard rectangular boxes. A consistent 16:9 frame will provide the missing compositional discipline.

The workflow guidance reinforces a top step rail, width-controlled operational zones, a real aside that shows live values, explicit commit actions, and live checks that state the actual value rather than a generic “OK”. For CAVE, the existing lifecycle rail remains meaningful because it represents a true sequence; interior numeric labels should be reserved for genuine ordered tasks.

The anti-cheap audit bans generic AI gradients, hard grey borders, mixed radii, fake-precise numbers, decorative numbered markers, overused technical typography and repetitive equal-card grids. CAVE will therefore keep its real measured values, reduce mono usage to metadata, replace the current box-by-box geometry with a primary-stage composition, and use a less generic display face for the entering page while retaining a legible sans for controls.

## Design Read

**Register:** entering page = brand; workbench = product/workflow. **Soul:** quiet institutional confidence with cinematic architectural scale. **Spectacle:** 5/10 on entry, 2/10 inside the tool. **Density:** 2/10 on entry, 8/10 inside the tool. **Core move:** one controlled 16:9 instrument frame, with a restrained atmospheric threshold before the operational console.
