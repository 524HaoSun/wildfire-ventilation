# CAVE Workbench — System UI Redesign Audit

## Review baseline

Reviewed at **1440×810** on Challenge, Design, Run and Analyse from checkpoint `62e55f2f`. The current direction remains an Editorial Research Atlas / Instrumented Architectural Modernism workbench, but implementation has drifted toward a pale, over-boxed software dashboard.

## System-level failures

The typography lacks a controlled scale. Several eyebrow labels, provenance labels, chart annotations and navigation labels are too small and too light, while selected measurements and Analyse dose numerals are disproportionately large. Uppercase is overused for descriptive headings that should read naturally. The hierarchy must be rebuilt around a limited, legible scale with sentence case for most UI language.

The palette is too low-contrast and inconsistent. Warm substrate, white cards and pale grey rules merge together, while teal appears as both process colour and generic fill. The revised palette must use a cleaner mineral-paper background, deep blue-black text, a restrained petrol blue for active processes, clay red only for measured smoke, and a single muted ochre for warnings. No component may rely on faint grey text for primary meaning.

The interface contains too many equal bordered cards. Each page needs one dominant scientific field, with supporting evidence presented as ruled ledgers, aligned annotations and quiet tonal bands rather than repeated boxes.

## Screen-specific findings

### 01 Challenge

The Challenge should compare two boundary conditions with genuinely different experimental value: **New York · June 2023** for long-range wildfire smoke and **London · July 2022** for compound heatwave and urban-edge fire exposure. Both event rows must share one neutral substrate and structural template; only the selected row may receive a controlled tinted wash and vertical accent. The main plot should dominate, while event metadata, the 24-hour slider and summary metrics form one coherent evidence plate.

### 02 Design

The architectural section is the strongest brand asset and should remain dominant. Control groups currently resemble generic segmented widgets; they should become calibrated protocol controls with clearer labels, stronger value contrast and fewer filled teal blocks. Prediction evidence should read as a single verification ledger rather than three competing cards.

### 03 Run

Playback controls currently consume too much visual authority while the spatial replay is pale and undersized. The cutaway must become the main evidence object. Sensors and charts should behave like annotations around the building. Playback controls should become one slim ruled strip, and the sensor rack needs stronger row rhythm without oversized values.

### 04 Analyse

The top three panels read as equal KPI cards and the dose numeral is too dominant. Ventilation Guidance must be the authored conclusion of the page; fit, exposure and dose are supporting evidence. The recommendation needs stronger hierarchy, clearer reading width and quieter evidence graphics.

## Independent visual-review contract

Preserve the centered 16:9 instrument stage, dark theatre surround, serif-first scientific language, architectural linework and measured charting. Reduce dashboard-card chrome, privilege one dominant field per screen, attach provenance and method directly to every major number, reserve teal for active process or decisive action, make Run architectural before procedural, and make Ventilation Guidance the clear editorial conclusion of Analyse.

## Acceptance criteria

Primary text must meet a visibly strong contrast against its actual background. No essential label may appear smaller than the revised minimum scale at 1440×810. Descriptive headings use sentence case; uppercase is restricted to short provenance codes, units and compact instrument identifiers. Event rows use identical neutral construction except for the selected state. Numerals must be sized by decision importance rather than novelty. All four pages must fit without collision at 1440×810 and 1920×1080.
